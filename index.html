about me

Hi! My name is Justin D'Souza and I am a Computer Engineering student at the University of Waterloo with strong interests in machine learning and finance.


Although I was born and raised in Canada, I've lived in Hong Kong, India, and the Philippines; this has enabled me to appreciate cultural differences and broaden my outlook on life.


More recently, as I've been working as an Innovation Software Engineer at Manulife's Lab of Forward Thinking, I've become increasingly fascinated with machine learning from a financial services standpoint. Big data is inherent in the finance industry, and because of this, I believe that probablistic machine learning approaches are critical for successfully implementing investment and trading strategies. In some of my personal projects, I've utilized statistical approaches including linear regression, k-NN and regression trees to model various stock trading situations.


Outside of coding, I enjoy playing chess competitively at the national level: my greatest achievement to date has been placing 2nd at the 2016 Canadian University Chess Championships. I also play the clarinet and piano recreationally, and I've completed both my Grade 8 Clarinet and Grade 8 Piano practical exams at the Royal Conservatory of Music with Honours.

Machine Learning

In today's age, there is an immense amount of data available to us, but the trouble is in putting it all to work. In my role at Manulife, I explore and implement deep learning architectures on a cognitive platform for Portfolio Managers to equip them with smart decision-making tools. This experience has shaped my belief, that if properly leveraged, machine learning models can offer innovative solutions to big data problems across every industry.


At Manulife, I was initially responsible for sourcing a portion of the financial data that was going to be fed into a neural net for text analysis. It was fun scraping data from platforms like Morningstar and coding up Python and Node.js scripts to wrap different Natural Language Processing APIs. After all the data was sourced, I started getting into the good stuff...


I began creating different ML models with Google's TensorFlow. I implemented a word2vec (skip-gram) model with hundreds of data files to visualize the learned embeddings, and even wrote a recurrent neural net from scratch! (Check out my resume for the sweet deets!)
