about me

Hey! My name is Justin D'Souza and I'm a Computer Engineering student at the University of Waterloo with strong interests in machine learning and quantitative finance. I currently work at Quandl as a Junior Data Scientist on the Alternative Data team.


A few fun facts about me. I was born and raised in Canada, but I've lived in Hong Kong, India, and the Philippines. I developed some conversational fluency in Mandarin while in Hong Kong and somehow retained most of what I learned.


Most recently, I was at CPP Investment Board as an Intern Analyst on the Credit trading desk within the Global Capital Markets division. Before that, I worked as Software Engineering intern at CI Investments Inc., where I developed dynamic web applications for security analysis and report automation. I've also worked as a Data Science Intern at Manulife's Lab of Forward Thinking, where I focused on deep learning for NLP with financial time-series data.


Outside of coding, I relish playing chess competitively at the national level: I've placed among the top 10 in my age group individually and currently lead UWaterloo's chess team in intercollegiate tournaments. I also play the clarinet and piano recreationally, and I've completed both my Grade 8 Clarinet and Grade 8 Piano practical exams at the Royal Conservatory of Music with Honours.

Machine Learning

In today's age, there is an immense amount of data available to us, but the trouble is in putting it all to work. In my role at Manulife, I explored and implemented deep learning architectures on a cognitive platform for Portfolio Managers to equip them with smart decision-making tools. This experience shaped my belief, that if properly leveraged, machine learning models can offer innovative solutions to big data problems across every industry.


At Manulife, I was initially responsible for sourcing a portion of the financial data that was going to be fed into a neural net for text analysis. It was fun scraping data from platforms like Morningstar and coding up Python and Node.js scripts to wrap different Natural Language Processing APIs. After all the data was sourced, I started getting into the good stuff...


I began creating different ML models with Google's TensorFlow. I implemented a word2vec (skip-gram) model with thousands of data files to visualize the learned embeddings, and even wrote a recurrent neural net from scratch!
